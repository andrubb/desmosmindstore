# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Costa Rican gym-goers and streetwear buyers, roughly 18–35, browsing on phones. They follow international gym-wear culture (Gymshark, YoungLA, Darc Sport, Breath The Divinity, Ryderwear, Alphalete) but can't buy it easily: international shipping to CR is slow, expensive, and risky. Their job-to-be-done: find the piece they've seen on Instagram, know whether it's available **now in Costa Rica** or **by encargo (order-in)**, and close the purchase over WhatsApp — the default commerce channel in CR. Emotional goal on landing: intensity and exclusivity ("this is the real thing, and it's here"), followed immediately by trust ("these people actually deliver").

## Product Purpose

DESMOSMIND is a curated importer/storefront of premium international gym-wear and gothic streetwear for Costa Rica. Two buying paths: **Stock CR** (in-country, immediate) and **Encargo** (ordered from the US per drop cycle). The site's job is to (1) present drops like limited events, (2) make the two-path buying model instantly clear, and (3) convert into WhatsApp conversations. Success = WhatsApp orders and repeat drop-followers, not newsletter metrics.

## Brand Personality

Bold, premium, dark. Gothic streetwear energy — "Darc Sport meets YoungLA": black steel, blood red, bat iconography, discipline-as-aesthetic. The voice is declarative and Spanish-first ("ENTRENA. SANGRA. REPITE." energy), never cute, never corporate. Premium is expressed through restraint plus violence: vast black space, one weapon-red accent, hard chamfered edges, controlled glitch/scan motion. It should feel like a night gym in an abandoned cathedral, not a clothing catalog.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS product site**: no cream/beige minimalism, no gradient-text heroes, no metric-card grids, no soft rounded pastel anything.
- **Shopify/dropshipper template look**: no cluttered product walls, no fake-urgency popup stacks, no discount-sticker chaos. Scarcity here is staged like a campaign, not screamed.
- **Editorial-magazine affectation**: no display serifs, no italic Fraunces energy. This brand is condensed, uppercase, industrial.
- **Neon cyberpunk**: red is blood, not laser. No purple/cyan glows, no synthwave gradients.

## Design Principles

1. **Darkness is the canvas, red is the blade.** Near-black surfaces (#050505–#0e0e0e) carry everything; #DC1111 appears only where it cuts — CTAs, live-status, drops, glitch accents.
2. **Drops are events, not inventory.** Limited releases get cinematic treatment (countdowns, staged reveals); the standing catalog stays calm and shoppable.
3. **Mobile is the storefront.** CR buyers arrive from Instagram/WhatsApp on phones; every design decision is judged at 375px first, desktop second.
4. **Two paths, zero confusion.** Stock CR vs Encargo must be legible at card level, section level, and checkout level — a visitor should never wonder "can I have this now?"
5. **Every pixel earns trust.** An importer lives on credibility: real brand logos, real reviews, real WhatsApp human, clear delivery promises. Nothing that smells like a scam template.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA intent on a dark canvas: body text ≥4.5:1 against #050505 (the #d4d0c8 body ink passes), red-on-black reserved for large display text and non-text accents. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all loops and reveals (already partially implemented — every new animation must ship with one). Touch targets ≥44px on mobile. Spanish-first copy with EN toggle; icons never carry meaning alone.
